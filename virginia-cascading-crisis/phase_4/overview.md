# Phase 4: Forgotten Country

**Course Topic:** Political & Societal Security

**Time Period:** Day 11-15 of crisis (60 minutes real-time)

---

## Context

Phase 3 kept the region from collapsing outright, but it did not restore normal life. People waited in lines, watched fuel convoys pass their hollers, and listened to officials explain why some places got help first. In coalfield counties, anger has hardened into a narrative: "the state saves the cities and leaves us behind." In the New River Valley and Roanoke Valley, residents fear that disruption will return at any moment. Trust is no longer just a public relations issue; it is now an operational constraint.

The adversary adapts. As systems come back in fragments, AI-generated political content and conspiracy narratives flood the same channels used for emergency updates. Deepfakes and edited clips portray leaders as liars or tyrants. In tight-knit communities where offline networks matter, rumors move faster than official corrections. The question is no longer only "what is true?", but "who is allowed to decide?"

A flashpoint is forming. A protest in Wise County blocks US-23, threatening supply movement to the coalfields. Local officials in coalfield counties signal they will set their own distribution rules. Armed volunteer groups claim they are protecting their communities, while others report intimidation. If response leaders cannot manage legitimacy, de-escalate safely, and communicate consistently, Phase 5 will begin with a governance crisis that makes every health and environmental decision harder.

## Crisis Engine Analysis

### What just changed unexpectedly?
Political conflict becomes the primary driver of risk: protests, local defiance, and AI-enabled disinformation begin to interrupt the response itself. The response apparatus is now a contested actor in the story, not a neutral helper.

### Which system is now stressed?
- [x] Governance: contested authority, local defiance, legitimacy risk
- [x] Information: deepfakes, rumor networks, coordinated amplification
- [x] Infrastructure: roads and distribution corridors become protest targets
- [x] Health: staff threats and fear reduce service capacity
- [ ] Economy

### Who experiences immediate harm?
- [x] Individuals: residents caught at flashpoints, workers trying to reach jobs, families in queues
- [x] Vulnerable communities: rural households far from services, low-trust communities, people without connectivity
- [x] Institutions: local government, law enforcement, hospitals, utilities facing legitimacy attacks
- [x] Cross-border populations: travelers and supply chains moving through I-81 corridor

### What decision must be made within limited time?
**De-escalation vs. coercion, and transparency vs. control.** Teams must decide how to keep corridors open and prevent violence while maintaining civil liberties and public legitimacy.

**Deadline:** Multiple flashpoints (US-23 blockade, distribution rumors, town hall) can turn violent within 10-15 minutes of play time if unmanaged.

### What future risk is created by this decision?
- Over-enforcement can delegitimize the response and trigger broader defiance.
- Under-enforcement can allow violence, intimidation, and supply disruption to spread.
- Over-transparency can create short-term panic if risks are communicated poorly.
- Over-control can fuel conspiracy narratives and undermine compliance.

### Who makes the decision, and who is left out?
- Decision makers: Governor's Liaison, local government, EMA, Sheriff's Coalition, VDOT, core communications partners
- Stakeholders excluded: rural residents without representation, exhausted frontline workers, marginalized groups targeted by harassment
- Why this matters: excluded groups become recruitment pools for rumor networks and escalation, and they bear disproportionate harm.

### Team-Specific Objectives

**Governor's SW Virginia Liaison:**
- Establish a state-local governance compact that reduces public infighting and clarifies approvals
- Deploy mediation and listening resources before flashpoints become violence
- Coordinate a coherent narrative for federal engagement (hearings, funding pathways, evidence)

**Montgomery County Government:**
- Run safe, rule-based public forums that reduce rumor-driven anger
- Put emergency ground rules in place for distribution sites and traffic safety
- Coordinate with enforcement and community leaders to prevent town hall violence

**Shentel Communications:**
- Improve alerting reliability while avoiding rumor amplification
- Coordinate rapid takedown and labeling for high-impact deepfakes
- Provide a rumor hotline pipeline that feeds fact-check and response teams

**SW Virginia Sheriff's Coalition:**
- Prioritize de-escalation and liaison work to avoid viral escalation footage
- Keep critical corridors and distribution sites safe with targeted, accountable enforcement
- Prevent armed intimidation while minimizing legitimacy loss

**VA DEQ SW Regional Office:**
- Rapidly validate or debunk contamination claims with credible evidence
- Communicate environmental status with clarity about knowns/unknowns
- Support enforcement decisions with technical facts (water, ash, air)

**WDBJ7 News:**
- Operate a rapid fact-check desk and repeatable rumor rebuttals
- Host credible expert forums that reduce conspiracy traction
- Coordinate messaging timing with EMA and state/local leaders to avoid contradictions

**Virginia Tech Crisis Response:**
- Map rumor spread and identify high-risk nodes and flashpoints
- Provide analysis to support de-escalation planning and resource transparency
- Support federal engagement with concise evidence packages and economic/social impact framing

**VDOT SW Region:**
- Keep US-23, I-81, and key routes usable for emergency movement and supply chains
- Implement detours, signage, and safe separation between crowds and convoys
- Coordinate any priority convoy lanes with appropriate approvals

**Carilion Clinic System:**
- Protect staff and facilities from targeted threats and harassment
- Expand mental health surge capacity in coordination with community partners
- Provide trusted health messaging to counter fear-driven rumors

**CISA Regional Coordinator:**
- Provide disinformation threat briefs and recommended countermeasures
- Protect critical civic systems that could be targeted for politically timed sabotage
- Coordinate technical response to deepfake campaigns with Shentel and media partners

**Appalachian Faith & Community Network:**
- Activate trusted messenger networks that reach offline communities
- Provide safe havens and support services to reduce fear and panic
- Support de-escalation at sites without becoming a partisan actor

**United Way SW Virginia:**
- Stand up structured mediation and grievance intake with public tracking of commitments
- Deploy trained volunteers to reduce conflict at public sites
- Coordinate community-level supports that prevent anger from becoming violence

**SW Virginia EMA:**
- Stand up joint information operations and consistent public update cadence
- Implement public alerting and rumor rebuttals aligned with credible messengers
- Publish transparent resource allocation reporting to reduce favoritism narratives

**Food City / Regional Supply Chain:**
- Publish and enforce fair distribution rules that are understandable and auditable
- Use escorted mobile distribution to reach underserved areas safely
- Create an advisory mechanism to reduce conflict and perceived favoritism

**AEP Appalachian Power:**
- Increase legitimacy by publishing restoration priorities with clear criteria
- Protect critical substations from sabotage amid heightened tensions
- Provide community support sites (charging, heating/cooling) that reduce frustration

## Constraints

### Time Constraints
- Phase duration: 60 minutes real-time = Days 11-15 simulated
- Critical decision deadlines: 0:10-0:15 for protest/rumor flashpoints

### Budget Constraints
- Budgets carry over from Phase 3 end state
- Legitimacy-building actions are relatively low-cost but require coordination
- High-cost actions (grid hardening, mobile support sites) trade off against later phases

### Resource Constraints
- Law enforcement and healthcare staff are exhausted; surge capacity is limited
- Transportation corridors are vulnerable to disruption
- Communications bandwidth is constrained by rumor volume and fragmented channels

### Information Constraints
- Known: public frustration is high, protests are forming, deepfakes are circulating
- Unknown: which narratives are coordinated vs organic, where the next flashpoint will emerge
- Unreliable: viral claims about contamination, arrests, and "secret priorities"

### Political/Legal Constraints
- Protest response must balance public safety with First Amendment rights
- Surveillance and enforcement choices have long-term legitimacy consequences
- State-local conflict can paralyze approvals unless a compact is created
